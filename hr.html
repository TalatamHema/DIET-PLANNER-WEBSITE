Avocado Salad

This refreshing avocado salad is packed with nutrients and flavor. It's quick and easy to make, perfect for a healthy lunch or side dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh cilantro, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon lime juice
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine diced avocados, cherry tomatoes, red onion, and cilantro.
  2. Drizzle olive oil and lime juice over the salad.
  3. Gently toss until well combined.
  4. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  5. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Quinoa Salad

This protein-packed quinoa salad is perfect for a light lunch or dinner. It's loaded with colorful vegetables and tossed in a tangy vinaigrette dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring water to a boil. Add quinoa, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until water is absorbed and quinoa is cooked.
  2. Let quinoa cool completely.
  3. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, diced bell peppers, cucumber, red onion, and parsley.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, red wine vinegar, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  5. Pour dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
  6. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving.

Broccoli & Cheese Omelet

This bright-green omelet is a perfect way to get kids to eat their veggies.

Ingredients:

  • 2 teaspoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ cup finely chopped broccoli
  • ¼ cup finely chopped spinach
  • 1 large egg
  • 1 tablespoon reduced-fat milk
  • 2 tablespoons shredded Monterey Jack cheese
  • ⅛ teaspoon salt
  • 1 tablespoon reduced-fat sour cream
  • 1 tablespoon finely chopped chives

Instructions:

  1. Heat oil in a small nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add broccoli and spinach and cook, stirring occasionally, until bright green and tender, 2 to 4 minutes.
  3. Meanwhile, whisk egg and milk in a small bowl.
  4. Add the mixture to the pan and stir briefly to combine with the vegetables.
  5. Cook, tilting the pan and letting egg run under the edges, until the egg forms a thin, even layer.
  6. Continue to cook, reducing the heat if starting to brown, until just slightly wet, 1 to 2 minutes.
  7. Sprinkle with cheese and salt. Use a spatula to roll into an omelet.
  8. Serve topped with sour cream and chives.

Vegetable Stir-Fry

This vegetable stir-fry is a colorful and nutritious dish that's quick and easy to prepare. It's loaded with a variety of fresh vegetables and flavored with a savory sauce.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, carrots, snap peas)
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger to make the sauce.
  2. In a separate bowl, dissolve cornstarch in water to make a slurry.
  3. Heat a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  4. Pour the sauce over the vegetables and toss to coat evenly.
  5. Add the cornstarch slurry to the skillet and stir until the sauce thickens.
  6. Remove from heat and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  7. Serve the vegetable stir-fry hot over cooked rice or noodles. Enjoy!

Baked Salmon with Asparagus

This baked salmon with asparagus is a healthy and delicious meal that's perfect for busy weeknights. It's seasoned with garlic, lemon, and herbs, and baked to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Place salmon fillets on the prepared baking sheet. Arrange asparagus around the salmon.
  3. In a small bowl, whisk together minced garlic, olive oil, dried thyme, salt, and pepper.
  4. Brush the garlic herb mixture over the salmon and asparagus.
  5. Place lemon slices on top of the salmon.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 12-15 minutes, or until salmon is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  7. Remove from the oven and serve immediately. Enjoy!

Mediterranean Chickpea Salad

This Mediterranean chickpea salad is bursting with flavors and textures. It's loaded with chickpeas, fresh vegetables, and herbs, all tossed in a zesty lemon dressing.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cans (15 oz each) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cucumber, diced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup red onion, finely chopped
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, sliced
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a large bowl, combine chickpeas, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, red onion, olives, and parsley.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, dried oregano, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to coat evenly.
  4. If using, sprinkle crumbled feta cheese over the salad.
  5. Refrigerate for at least 30 minutes before serving to allow the flavors to meld.
  6. Serve chilled and enjoy!

Veggie Quinoa Bowl

This veggie quinoa bowl is a nutritious and satisfying meal that's easy to customize with your favorite vegetables and toppings. It's packed with protein, fiber, and vitamins.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, zucchini, carrots)
  • 1/2 cup cooked chickpeas or black beans
  • 1 avocado, sliced
  • 1/4 cup hummus
  • 2 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring vegetable broth or water to a boil. Add quinoa, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until liquid is absorbed and quinoa is cooked.
  2. Fluff quinoa with a fork and let it cool slightly.
  3. Divide cooked quinoa among serving bowls. Top with mixed vegetables, chickpeas or black beans, and avocado slices.
  4. In a small bowl, whisk together hummus, tahini, lemon juice, olive oil, garlic powder, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  5. Drizzle dressing over the quinoa bowls.
  6. Optional: garnish with fresh herbs or sesame seeds.
  7. Serve the veggie quinoa bowls immediately and enjoy!

Spinach and Feta Stuffed Chicken Breast

This spinach and feta stuffed chicken breast is a flavorful and satisfying dish that's perfect for a special dinner. It's easy to make and sure to impress!

Ingredients:

  • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 2 cups fresh spinach leaves
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon paprika
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish with olive oil or non-stick spray.
  2. Cut a slit in the side of each chicken breast to create a pocket.
  3. In a small bowl, mix together spinach, feta cheese, minced garlic, olive oil, dried oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper.
  4. Stuff each chicken breast with the spinach and feta mixture, then secure with toothpicks if needed.
  5. Place stuffed chicken breasts in the prepared baking dish.
  6. Bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes, or until chicken is cooked through and no longer pink in the center.
  7. Remove toothpicks before serving. Enjoy your spinach and feta stuffed chicken breast!

Vegetable Lentil Soup

This vegetable lentil soup is hearty, healthy, and perfect for chilly days. It's loaded with nutritious vegetables, protein-packed lentils, and flavorful herbs and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dry green or brown lentils, rinsed
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, combine lentils, vegetable broth, carrots, celery, onion, garlic, diced tomatoes (with juices), dried thyme, dried oregano, bay leaf, salt, and pepper.
  2. Bring soup to a boil, then reduce heat to low and simmer for 25-30 minutes, or until lentils and vegetables are tender.
  3. Remove bay leaf from the soup.
  4. Taste and adjust seasoning if needed.
  5. Serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if desired. Enjoy your vegetable lentil soup!

Grilled Vegetable Quinoa Salad

This grilled vegetable quinoa salad is bursting with flavor and nutrients. It's made with grilled vegetables, protein-rich quinoa, and a tangy balsamic vinaigrette.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as zucchini, bell peppers, mushrooms, onions)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil leaves for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a saucepan, bring vegetable broth or water to a boil. Add quinoa, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until liquid is absorbed and quinoa is cooked.
  2. Preheat grill to medium-high heat.
  3. In a bowl, toss mixed vegetables with olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Grill vegetables for 5-7 minutes per side, or until tender and slightly charred.
  5. In a small bowl, whisk together balsamic vinegar, Dijon mustard, minced garlic, salt, and pepper to make the dressing.
  6. Combine cooked quinoa and grilled vegetables in a large bowl. Drizzle with the balsamic vinaigrette and toss to coat.
  7. Garnish with fresh basil leaves before serving. Enjoy your grilled vegetable quinoa salad!

Vegetable Curry with Chickpeas

This vegetable curry with chickpeas is a flavorful and satisfying dish that's perfect for a cozy dinner. It's packed with a variety of vegetables, chickpeas, and aromatic spices.

Ingredients:

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground turmeric
  • 1 can (14 oz) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz) coconut milk
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as carrots, potatoes, bell peppers, peas)
  • 1 can (14 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and minced garlic, and sauté until softened.
  2. Add curry powder, cumin, and turmeric to the pot, and stir until fragrant.
  3. Stir in diced tomatoes (with juices), coconut milk, mixed vegetables, and chickpeas. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Bring the curry to a simmer, then reduce heat to low and cover. Let simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until vegetables are tender.
  5. Adjust seasoning if needed. Serve hot, garnished with fresh cilantro. Enjoy your vegetable curry with chickpeas!

Caprese Stuffed Avocado

This caprese stuffed avocado is a simple and elegant appetizer or light meal. It's filled with creamy avocado, juicy tomatoes, fresh mozzarella, and aromatic basil.

Ingredients:

  • 2 ripe avocados, halved and pitted
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/2 cup fresh mozzarella balls, halved
  • 2 tablespoons balsamic glaze
  • 2 tablespoons fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Scoop out a small portion of avocado flesh from each avocado half to create a larger cavity.
  2. In a bowl, combine cherry tomatoes, mozzarella balls, chopped basil, salt, and pepper.
  3. Divide the tomato and mozzarella mixture evenly among the avocado halves.
  4. Drizzle balsamic glaze over the stuffed avocados.
  5. Serve immediately as a delicious and healthy appetizer or light meal. Enjoy your caprese stuffed avocado!

Teriyaki Tofu Stir-Fry

This teriyaki tofu stir-fry is a delicious and satisfying dish that's packed with protein and vegetables. It's flavored with a homemade teriyaki sauce and served over rice or noodles.

Ingredients:

  • 1 block (14 oz) extra firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (such as bell peppers, broccoli, snap peas)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey or maple syrup
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon fresh ginger, grated
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch
  • 2 tablespoons water
  • Sesame seeds and sliced green onions for garnish
  • Cooked rice or noodles for serving

Instructions:

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey or maple syrup, minced garlic, and grated ginger to make the teriyaki sauce.
  2. In another small bowl, dissolve cornstarch in water to make a slurry.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Add cubed tofu and stir-fry until golden brown on all sides.
  4. Add mixed vegetables to the skillet and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until tender-crisp.
  5. Pour the teriyaki sauce over the tofu and vegetables. Stir to coat evenly.
  6. Add the cornstarch slurry to the skillet and stir until the sauce thickens.
  7. Remove from heat and garnish with sesame seeds and sliced green onions.
  8. Serve hot over cooked rice or noodles. Enjoy your teriyaki tofu stir-fry!

Grilled Shrimp and Vegetable Skewers

These grilled shrimp and vegetable skewers are perfect for a summer barbecue or outdoor gathering. They're marinated in a flavorful herb and garlic marinade and grilled to perfection.

Ingredients:

  • 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 2 bell peppers (any color), cut into chunks
  • 1 red onion, cut into chunks
  • 1 zucchini, sliced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Wooden or metal skewers

Instructions:

  1. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for at least 30 minutes to prevent burning.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, minced garlic, lemon juice, dried oregano, dried thyme, salt, and pepper to make the marinade.
  3. Thread the shrimp, bell peppers, onion, and zucchini onto the skewers, alternating between ingredients.
  4. Place the skewers in a shallow dish and pour the marinade over them, turning to coat evenly. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to marinate.
  5. Preheat grill to medium-high heat. Lightly oil the grill grates.
  6. Remove the skewers from the marinade and discard any excess marinade.
  7. Grill the skewers for 2-3 minutes per side, or until shrimp are pink and vegetables are tender and slightly charred.
  8. Remove from the grill and serve immediately. Enjoy your grilled shrimp and vegetable skewers!

Quinoa Stuffed Bell Peppers

These quinoa stuffed bell peppers are a wholesome and satisfying meal that's packed with protein and fiber. They're filled with a flavorful mixture of quinoa, black beans, corn, and spices.

Ingredients:

  • 4 large bell peppers (any color)
  • 1 cup quinoa, rinsed
  • 2 cups vegetable broth or water
  • 1 can (15 oz) black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 1 cup corn kernels (fresh or frozen)
  • 1/2 cup diced tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup diced onion
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup shredded cheese (cheddar, Monterey Jack, etc.)
  • Fresh cilantro for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C). Grease a baking dish large enough to hold the bell peppers.
  2. Cut the tops off the bell peppers and remove the seeds and membranes from inside.
  3. In a saucepan, bring vegetable broth or water to a boil. Add quinoa, cover, and simmer for 15-20 minutes until liquid is absorbed and quinoa is cooked.
  4. In a large bowl, combine cooked quinoa, black beans, corn, diced tomatoes, diced onion, minced garlic, ground cumin, chili powder, salt, and pepper. Mix well to combine.
  5. Stuff each bell pepper with the quinoa mixture, packing it tightly.
  6. Place stuffed bell peppers in the prepared baking dish. Cover with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 25-30 minutes.
  7. Remove foil, sprinkle shredded cheese over the tops of the stuffed peppers, and return to the oven. Bake for an additional 5-10 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Remove from the oven and let cool slightly before serving. Garnish with fresh cilantro if desired. Enjoy your quinoa stuffed bell peppers!
